Paragraphs

Lead Text

Lead text should be set in Source Sans Pro 300 4.5rem white.

.lead 4.5rem 300

Lead text risus eget urna mollis ornare vel eu leo. Cum sociis natoque penatibus et magnis dis parturient montes, nascetur ridiculus mus. Nullam id dolor id nibh ultricies vehicula.

<p class="lead">lead text</p>

Lead Text > strong

The strong element represents strong importance, seriousness, or urgency for its contents.

Lead text with strong text in the middle.

<p class="lead">Lead text with <strong>strong text</strong> in the middle.</p>

Lead Text > em

The em element represents stress emphasis of its contents.

Lead text with emphasized text in the middle.

<p class="lead">Lead text with <em>emphasized text</em> in the middle.</p>

Lead Text > small

The small element represents side comments such as small print.

Lead text with small text in the middle.

<p class="lead">Lead text with <small>small text</small> in the middle.</p>

Other Text Sizes

Below is a list of old common.css text-size variations and suggested replacements. When deciding how to size text, keep the following in mind:

  • Avoid overrides of heading styles to increase consistency between apps.
  • Use app-specific classes to add new styles or customize existing styles
  • If a size reduction is necessary, try the small element to reduce type by approximately 1/6th.
  • Use upper- and lowercase letters except in H6.
  • Submit typographic improvements through Trac.
